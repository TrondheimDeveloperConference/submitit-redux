Got something to say? JavaZone is the place to say it!

JavaZone takes place in Oslo, Norway, on September 7th-8th 2016. The conference offers a combination of technical talks and workshops in an informal atmosphere with an expected attendance of more than 2500 highly skilled developers.

Submitting a talk to JavaZone 2016

Submitting: Once you have submitted your talk you will receive a confirmation by email. In this email you'll find a link to a page where you can further update your submission should you wish.

Publication: The information given in this form will be published in the program, unless specifically stated otherwise.

Language: Please complete this form using the same language that you'll be presenting in. In other words, if you plan on presenting in Norwegian, skriv på norsk!

Deadline: The deadline for submissions is April 18th 2016, but feel free to submit your talk early :-)

Your talk

Title

Select an expressive and snappy title that captures the content of your talk without being too long. Remember that the title must be attractive and should make people curious.

Full abstract

Give a concise description of the content and goals of your talk. Try not to exceed 300 words, as shorter and more to-the-point descriptions are more likely to be read by the participants.

Expected audience and code level

Who should attend this session? How will the participants benefit from attending? Explicitly state what your audience needs to know in order to get the most out of your presentation. To indicate the amount of code in your talk we recommend you use the following phrases: "No code", "Code in slides" or "Live coding".
