Submit your presentation or workshop for JavaZone 2014 here!

Ready to submit? Let us give you a little bit of information first

  • When you submit, you will receive a confirmation by email. There you will find a link to a page where you can update your submission.
  • The information given in this form will be used publicly in the program, unless specified otherwise.
  • Please fill in this form in the language you are giving your talk. That means, if you are presenting in Norwegian, skriv på norsk!
  • Deadline for submissions is 28. April 2014, but feel free to submit your talk early :-)
  • Now that you know all this, go ahead and fill out this form.

Submissions are now closed

Call for speakers are now closed. If you need to submit please contact us at program@java.no and you will get the password needed to submit.

Title

Select an expressive title that captures the contents of your talk well without being too long. Remember that the title must be attractive and should make people curious.

Summary

Describe the content of your talk in a few sentences. The summary will be used as an introduction to your talk in the program. It might also be used in printed material, on Twitter, at Vimeo etc.

Full abstract

Give a concise description of the contents and goals of your talk. It should preferably not be longer than 300 words. Don't repeat the summary here, as the summary will be shown above this abstract in the program.

Language

Which language will you present your talk in? The slides may be in English even if you present the talk in Norwegian. We generally recommend that you hold the talk in the language you are most comfortable with.

Expected audience and code level

Who should attend this session? How will the participants benefit from attending? Clearly state what knowledge you expect from your participants. To indicate code level we recommend you use the following levels "No code", "Code in slides" or "Live coding".

Expected knowledge level

What level will you present at, and what do you thereby expect the listeners to know beforehand.

Outline (not public)

This information will be used by the program committee for reviewing the detailed contents. The outline should be rough agenda for the talk, with a few keywords for each section, and with a rough estimate of the time spent on each.

Please specify any additional special equipment you may need, e.g., more than one slide projector, video projection facilities, computer tables, paper boards, etc.
